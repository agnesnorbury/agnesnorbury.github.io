Agnes Norbury

My fundamental research interest is in elucidating the neural mechanisms underlying motivational and decision-making processes involved in clinically relevant aspects of human cognition – in particular in psychiatric disorders (addictive and affective disorders) and other conditions such as chronic pain.

I am currently based at the Computational and Biological Learning Laboratory at the University of Cambridge, where I work with Dr. Ben Seymour, building computational models of individual differences in aversive learning processes that have been identifed as vulnerability factors in chronic pain and anxiety disorders.
